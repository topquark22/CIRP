[2004-05-16] A note regarding CIRP and the SPEWS anti-spam service

It has come to our attention that mail from CIRP.org is being blocked by the SPEWS blackhole list (anti-spam service). A minority of e-mail hosting ISPs subscribe to that service and, as a consequence, if your ISP is one of these, you will be unable to receive mail from us. We would like to explain the situation and assure you that CIRP is opposed to spam. We do not send spam or support it in any way.

Our generous hosting provider, Tilted Planet, has a strict policy against spam on their network. In 2004, one of Tilted's former clients was accused of sending spam to advertise their site. This alleged spam did not come from Tilted's network; it was sent from somewhere else. Unfortunately, SPEWS unilaterally decided to blacklist the entire tilted.net network, which also hosts many diverse commercial, informational and non-profit sites.

Even though Tilted reported the spammer, all sites hosted here are still blacklisted by SPEWS. This is obviously unfair to the many quality sites who share this network.

If you are an e-mail hosting ISP, on behalf of your subscribers, we urge you to unblock e-mail from our legitimate site, cirp.org. If you use a blackhole list (DNSBL), there are many to choose from. You might consider choosing one that is less zealous and arbitrary than SPEWS.

Please bear with us in the meantime as we work to get our quality site removed from the SPEWS blacklist as soon as possible. Thank you for your understanding.

--CIRP
